Couscous

Description

Algerian couscous is a celebrated dish that captures the essence of North African cuisine. This traditional meal features light, fluffy grains of couscous, which are steamed to perfection and often served with a rich, savory stew. The stew typically includes a variety of vegetables such as carrots, zucchini, potatoes, and tomatoes, creating a colorful and nutritious medley. Protein sources like lamb, chicken, or beef are commonly added, infusing the dish with deep, hearty flavors.

Spices play a pivotal role in Algerian couscous, with a blend of cumin, coriander, cinnamon, and paprika providing warmth and depth. The dish is often garnished with fresh herbs like cilantro or parsley, adding a burst of freshness. Whether enjoyed during family gatherings or festive occasions, Algerian couscous is a testament to the region's culinary heritage, offering a comforting and flavorful experience that is both nourishing and satisfying.

Ingredients

  • Â¼ cup olive oil, or more as needed, divided
  • 8 mutton chops, fat removed
  • 4 chicken drumsticks
  • 1 pinch salt and ground black pepper to taste
  • 3 onions, quartered
  • water to cover
  • 2 tablespoons ground turmeric
  • 2 tablespoons ground cumin
  • 2 tablespoons ground coriander
  • 3 potatoes, cut into chunks
  • 3 turnips, cut into chunks
  • 3 carrots, sliced lengthwise and cut into chunks
  • 1 (6 ounce) can tomato paste
  • 2 tablespoons ras el hanout
  • 1 (7 ounce) can chickpeas, drained
  • 2 zucchini, sliced lengthwise and cut into chunks
  • 5 sprigs cilantro, chopped

Couscous:

  • 3 cups water
  • 2 cups couscous
  • 1 tablespoon butter
  • 3 tablespoons harissa

Steps

  1. Scrape the bottom of the pot with a wooden spoon to release browned bits. Return mutton chops and chicken to the pot. Pour in enough water to cover; add turmeric, cumin, and coriander. Cover and bring to a boil. Reduce heat to medium; simmer for 20 minutes.
  2. Stir potatoes, turnips, and carrots into the pot. Simmer, covered, until vegetables start to soften, about 10 minutes. Mix in tomato paste and ras el hanout; cook for 10 minutes. Stir in chickpeas, zucchini, and cilantro; continue cooking, covered, until zucchini is tender, about 5 minutes.
  3. Bring 3 cups water to a boil in a saucepan; remove from heat and stir in couscous and butter. Cover saucepan and let stand until water is absorbed completely, 5 to 10 minutes. Fluff couscous with a fork and stir in 1 tablespoon olive oil. Transfer to a serving dish.
  4. Ladle 2 scoops of cooking liquid into a bowl; mix in harissa until smooth.
  5. Scoop vegetables onto a serving plate. Scoop mutton and chicken onto a separate plate. Serve alongside couscous, harissa sauce, and remaining cooking liquid in the pot.
